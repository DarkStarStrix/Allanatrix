Nexa_Sci_distilled_Falcon-10B

Complete

A Falcon3-10B QLoRA model tuned for scientific question answering, hypothesis generation, and reproducible lab methodology drafting—trained on a 100k-sample synthetic corpus distilled via NexaCompute.

  • Base model: tiiuae/Falcon3-10B-Base with 4-bit NF4 adapters (rank 64, bf16 precision).
  • Dataset: 100k verified SFT samples (`sft_scientific_v1`) distilled from GPT-4/GPT-5-Mini across biology, materials science, and physics.
  • Training: 2 × A100 80GB (Prime Intellect), ~10 hours, ≈ $9 USD, validation/test loss 0.410 / 0.413.
  • Artifacts: merged safetensors, tokenizer, llm.txt, and pending rubric-based evaluation parquet.

NexaMol

Active

Spectrum-to-Structure Foundation Model

NexaMol is a molecular foundation model trained on more than 550 million mass spectra, designed to predict molecular structure directly from raw MS/MS data. This closes one of the largest gaps in scientific machine learning: converting the world's most abundant biochemical measurement into actionable structural knowledge.

  • Why This Matters: Mass spectrometry is the backbone of drug discovery, metabolomics, diagnostics, synthetic biology, and materials science. Billions of spectra are generated every year, yet >95% of molecules remain unidentified because structure prediction still depends on manual workflows, fragile heuristics, or slow search-based tools.
  • Impact: A generalizable model that can infer structure from spectra unlocks automatic compound identification, ultra-fast metabolite annotation, scalable chemical intelligence, accelerated R&D across biotech and pharma, and universal molecular embeddings for downstream models.
  • Vision: NexaMol aims to become the foundation layer for molecular ML, similar to how vision and language models became the foundation layers for their domains.
  • Features: Structure prediction from raw MS/MS, universal chemical embeddings, retrieval + annotation automation. Designed for drug discovery, metabolomics, and synthetic biology.
